Car-T

CAR-T (Chimeric Antigen Receptor T) is an advanced treatment approach used for certain types of cancer. It involves modifying a patient's own immune components to recognize and attack cancer.

CAR-T treatment has shown significant promise in treating various blood cancers, including:
  • Acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL)
  • Diffuse large B lymphoma (DLBCL)
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)
How it Works

A sample is taken from the patient's blood to extract specific immune components.

1

The extracted immune components are genetically modified in a lab to produce a chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) on their surface.

2

The CAR is designed to recognize a specific protein on abnormal growth

3

The modified immune components are expanded in number and then infused back into the patient's bloodstream.

4

The CAR-T recognize and bind to the cancer, leading to their destruction.

What are the risks and side effects?

Like all treatments, CAR T treatment has potential risks, but these are carefully managed by our medical teams:

  • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): A common side effect where patients experience flu-like symptoms, such as fever and low blood pressure. It is treatable with medication and monitoring.

  • Neurotoxicity:Temporary confusion, memory issues, or difficulty speaking may occur in some patients.

  • Fatigue and Weakness: General fatigue is common during recovery.

If you or someone you know is considering CAR-T treatment, it's essential to discuss the potential benefits and risks with a qualified healthcare professional.

What makes CAR-T treatment a breakthrough?

CAR-T treatment represents a shift in cancer care, moving from generic treatments to precision medicine. By harnessing the body's immune system, it opens doors to more effective and less toxic cancer treatments. Researchers are continuously improving the technology, aiming to expand its application to solid tumours and reduce its cost.
